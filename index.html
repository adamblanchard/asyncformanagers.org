Meetings as a last resort

Meetings are really expensive. Whenever you need to talk to someone or share some info or collaborate on an idea, always ask yourself how you can do it in an asynchronous way first. Once those options are exhausted, a meeting might be the last resort.

Sync time is limited, and it's not just yours to use up, so use it well

Sync time or meetings are not evil, and sometimes they can be useful. But, you have a very limited amount of sync time in the work week. If you ask people you work with how many hours they would genuinely choose to be in meetings each week, it will likely be a very small number. So remember, when you force synchronous communication you are also taking up all that precious time from other people as well.

Overcommunicate in public by default

Overcommunicating valuable because in async you can't rely on fast follow ups, so giving more context and considering what someone might ask straight away means you can already provide those answers.

Here's what you should include:
  1. Enough details to cover follow up questions - don't let it turn into a long delayed conversation.
  2. Response time expectations - when do you need an answer?
  3. As much context and supporting info as possible - documentation, tickets, screenshots, all that good stuff.
  4. A clear need and expectation - Are you looking for approval? Feedback? A decision? Tell them!

Go synchronous when it gets synchronous

If you end up having a real time discussion either by slack, email or something else, realise that you might be able to communicate more effectively on a call.

Lead by example

As a manager, the most effective way you can influence a successful asynchronous culture is to live by the values yourself. Don't ask a team to send public and async updates if you yourself call them into a meeting every time you need to tell them something.

Documentation is scalable

Writing something down scales knowledge. You don't have to keep having meetings to describe stuff to people, if you leave there is still some context around, and if new peoplejoin you don't have to remember and talk through every idea or decision you've ever made.
